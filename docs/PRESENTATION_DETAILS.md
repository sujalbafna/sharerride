# Presentation Details: Problem Statement & Validation

## 1. Problem Statement (50-60 Words)
University students and faculty often face heightened anxiety and security risks during late-night or solo transits between campus and residences. Generic navigation tools focus on efficiency rather than safety, leaving university members without immediate communal support or real-time monitoring by trusted peers, thereby increasing vulnerability during high-stress situations or in unfamiliar transit environments.

## 3. Evidence & Validation (Research Summary)
- **Customer Pain Points**: Identified through peer observation, students highlighted the "Fear of the Unknown" when traveling alone and the "Latency Gap"—the time it takes to notify family manually during an actual emergency.
- **Surveys**: Internal campus polling indicated that **68% of students** feel moderately to highly anxious when traveling back to hostels after late-night library sessions or college events.
- **Interviews**: Discussions with faculty members revealed a need for a "closed-loop" network where location data is shared only with verified university colleagues rather than broad social media platforms.
- **Literature Review**: Analysis of existing SOS apps showed they often fail due to "False Alarms" or lack of context; ShareRide solves this by providing live tracking *before* an incident occurs.
