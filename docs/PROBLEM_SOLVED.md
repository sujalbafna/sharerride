# Problems Solved by ShareRide

ShareRide is engineered to bridge the critical gaps left by generic navigation tools. Below are the primary problems the platform addresses:

## 1. The "Anxiety Gap" (Navigation vs. Safety)
- **The Problem**: Standard GPS apps (like Google Maps) are designed for efficiency. They tell you the shortest route but don't care if you are safe while walking it.
- **The Solution**: ShareRide transforms navigation into **Active Monitoring**. It provides the psychological comfort of "Virtual Companionship," knowing that a trusted friend is watching your progress in real-time.

## 2. The "Latency Gap" (Emergency Response Time)
- **The Problem**: In a high-stress situation, navigating through phone menus to call someone or send a location is too slow. Every second counts.
- **The Solution**: The **Zero-Latency SOS Protocol**. A single tap notifies the app network, and a 3-second hold automatically triggers a system-level SMS with precise GPS coordinates, bypassing data connectivity issues.

## 3. The "Trust Deficit" (Privacy vs. Visibility)
- **The Problem**: Users are often hesitant to use broad location-sharing apps (like Find My) because they don't want to be tracked 24/7 by everyone.
- **The Solution**: **Ephemeral Privacy**. Location sharing is strictly tied to an active journey. Once the trip is "Completed," tracking stops immediately. Furthermore, data is only visible to a user-defined "Trusted Circle" within the verified university community.

## 4. The "Isolation Factor" (Solo Transit Vulnerability)
- **The Problem**: Students traveling alone late at night (from labs or libraries) are statistically more vulnerable and feel more at risk.
- **The Solution**: **Companion Mode**. By broadcasting an itinerary, ShareRide facilitates "Safe Carpooling" or "Walking Groups," turning a solo transit into a communal security mesh.

## 5. The "Context Gap" (Information for Rescuers)
- **The Problem**: Standard emergency calls often lack context. Rescuers don't know the route, the vehicle details, or the intended destination.
- **The Solution**: **Full-Context Alerts**. When an alert is triggered, the network receives the rider's origin, destination, vehicle name (e.g., "White Swift"), and current live coordinates simultaneously.
